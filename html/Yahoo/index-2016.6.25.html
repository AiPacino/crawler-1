    'Vintage' Apple iPods reportedly rake in $20,000 on eBay

    Less than two years after the discontinuation of the iPod Classic, the "vintage" Apple music players are now considered collector's items - and they fetch the hefty sums on eBay, The Guardian reports. On the low end, a factory-sealed third-generation iPod shuffle can draw nearly $1,000 in online auctions, while a second-generation, still-boxed iPod classic with a moving wheel lists for almost $20,000, according to The Guardian. One memorabilia vendor told The Guardian there is growing demand for Apple-related goods, as collectors seek to build collections of every type of iPod ever made. Old iPod prices have spiked since the moment they were discontinued amid difficulty in getting the necessary parts and declining demand.
